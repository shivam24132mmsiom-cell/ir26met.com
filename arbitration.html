ARBITRATION

Arbitration is a method of dispute resolution in which the parties agree to submit their dispute to a neutral third party, called an arbitrator, who hears both sides and gives a binding decision that is enforceable by law

Process of Arbitration

  • Agreement – The parties agree to resolve disputes through arbitration, either through a clause in a contract or a separate agreement.
  • Notice of Arbitration – One party formally notifies the other of the dispute and initiates arbitration proceedings.
  • Appointment of Arbitrator– A neutral arbitrator or a panel of arbitrators is appointed by mutual consent or as per the agreement.
  • Preliminary Hearing– The arbitrator sets timelines, procedures, and rules for the arbitration process.
  • Submission of Statements– Both parties submit written statements, claims, defenses, and supporting evidence.
  • Hearings– The arbitrator hears arguments, examines evidence, and may question witnesses.
  • Arbitral Award– After considering all facts, the arbitrator delivers a final and binding decision.
  • Enforcement of Award– The arbitral award is enforceable like a court judgment under the law.

Arbitration Cases

Domestic Case

Air India Ltd. vs. Air India Employees Guild Arbitration Case (1985) - LANDMARK CASE

Parties

  • Plaintiff: Air India Ltd. (PSU - National Carrier)
  • Defendant: Air India Employees Guild (Recognized Union)
  • Location: Mumbai + Delhi (National Level Dispute)
  • Management: Chairman Mr. R. Venkitaramanan + PSU Counsel
  • Union: General Secretary Shri G. Ramanujam
  • Workers Involved: 18,000 Cabin Crew + Ground Staff

Reason for Dispute

The core issue was mass retrenchment and privatization fears, where Air India management planned a 25% workforce reduction affecting 4,500 jobs, while the union demanded no retrenchment without a Voluntary Retirement Scheme (VRS) and guaranteed job security. Additional demands included a long-pending wage revision since 1981 with a 40% hike, complete elimination of the contract labour system in the PSU, and one-time settlement packages for retiring staff. The dispute was triggered when Air India announced 1,200 immediate layoffs during a period of significant profit losses.

Dispute Timeline

  • Jan 15, 1985: Retrenchment notices issued → Union strike notice
  • Jan 22, 1985: All-India strike → 75% flights cancelled
  • Feb 1, 1985: Supreme Court intervention → Work notice
  • Feb 10, 1985: Tripartite conciliation (Central Labour Ministry)
  • Feb 15, 1985: Arbitration agreement signed (Sec 10A ID Act)
  • Mar 1-20, 1985: Arbitration hearings (6 sittings)
  • Apr 5, 1985: Landmark arbitration award published
  • Apr 10, 1985: Flights normalized
  • Loss to Company: ₹250 crore (45 days crisis)

Arbitration Details

  • Legal Basis: Section 10A Industrial Disputes Act 1947 + Air Corporation Act
  • Arbitrator: Shri Justice V. Balakrishna Eradi (Retd. Supreme Court Judge)
  • Agreement Date: February 15, 1985
  • Hearing Venue: Supreme Court Arbitration Centre, Delhi
  • Duration: 50 days (Feb 15 - Apr 5)
  • Parties Bound: Air India + 3 Unions (National Agreement)
  • Award Validity: 5 years (National Precedent)
  • Government Notified: Ministry of Civil Aviation

Case Processes

  • Supreme Court Intervention (February 1): The Supreme Court stepped in decisively, issuing a landmark interim order enforcing "No Work No Pay" during the strike period while mandating compulsory arbitration to resolve the dispute. Critically, the court banned any mass retrenchment, providing immediate job security to 18,000 employees and setting the stage for structured dispute resolution.
  • National Conciliation Proceedings (February 10): A grueling 15-hour marathon negotiation session was held under the Central Labour Ministry, marking the first breakthrough with a partial wage agreement that addressed immediate financial concerns. However, core issues of job security and retrenchment remained unresolved, necessitating formal arbitration.
  • Formal Arbitration Agreement (February 15):Both parties signed a binding arbitration agreement before the Chief Labour Commissioner, clearly defining the scope to cover retrenchment policies, pending wage revisions, and long-term job security guarantees. This agreement invoked Section 10A of the Industrial Disputes Act, making the process legally enforceable.
  • Comprehensive Arbitration Hearings (March 1-20):The arbitration spanned six detailed sittings over three weeks:
  • Sittings 1-2 (March 1-5):Air India management presented extensive financial statements justifying workforce rationalization.
  • Sittings 3-4 (March 10-15):The union countered with job security arguments and industry wage comparisons.
  • Sitting 5 (March 18):Independent expert economists provided testimony on PSU wage structures.
  • Sitting 6 (March 20):Final arguments were presented, completing the evidentiary phase.
  • Landmark Award Publication (April 5):Justice V. Balakrishna Eradi delivered a comprehensive 120-page arbitration award that became national precedent. The detailed judgement ordered nationwide implementation across Air India operations and set binding terms for all PSU aviation companies, effectively resolving the crisis and protecting 18,000 jobs.
